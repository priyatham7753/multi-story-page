Kabaddi

About Kabaddi:

Kabaddi is a contact team sport originating from ancient India, where two teams compete to score points by raiding into the opponent's territory and tagging as many defenders as possible without being tackled. The game is played on a rectangular court with two halves separated by a centerline. Each team takes turns sending a raider into the opponent's half, while the defenders attempt to stop the raider and prevent them from returning to their own half. Points are scored when a raider successfully tags opponents and returns to their own half without being tackled. Defenders score points by preventing the raider from returning or tackling them before they can tag opponents. Kabaddi requires speed, agility, and strength, making it a thrilling and intense sport to watch and play.

Rules of Kabaddi

  1. The game is played with two teams, each with seven players.
  2. One player from each team, known as the "raider," enters the opponent's half to tag as many defenders as possible.
  3. The raider must do so while chanting "kabaddi" repeatedly to show they're not inhaling during the raid.
  4. Defenders must try to stop the raider by tackling and holding them before they return to their half.
  5. Points are awarded for successful raids and defenses.
  6. The team with the most points at the end of the game wins.

Equipment for Kabaddi

Kabaddi players typically wear comfortable clothing, such as shorts and T-shirts, and use minimal protective gear, like knee and elbow pads.
